Understanding the Iris Dataset

Explore the basic statistics and distribution of the famous Iris flower dataset used in machine learning.

Dataset Overview

Total Samples: 150

Features: 4 (Sepal Length, Sepal Width, Petal Length, Petal Width)

Species: 3 (Setosa, Versicolor, Virginica)

Source: Collected by Edgar Anderson in 1935

Use Case: Classic example for classification algorithms

Species Distribution

Each species has exactly 50 samples (33.3% each)

Feature Statistics

Sepal Length

  • Mean: 5.84 cm
  • Std: 0.83 cm
  • Min: 4.30 cm
  • Max: 7.90 cm

Sepal Width

  • Mean: 3.06 cm
  • Std: 0.44 cm
  • Min: 2.00 cm
  • Max: 4.40 cm

Petal Length

  • Mean: 3.76 cm
  • Std: 1.77 cm
  • Min: 1.00 cm
  • Max: 6.90 cm

Petal Width

  • Mean: 1.20 cm
  • Std: 0.76 cm
  • Min: 0.10 cm
  • Max: 2.50 cm

Key Insights

• Setosa is linearly separable from the other two species, making it the easiest to classify.

• Versicolor and Virginica show some overlap, especially in sepal measurements, creating classification challenges.

• Petal measurements are more discriminative than sepal measurements for distinguishing species.

• The dataset's balance and small size make it perfect for demonstrating machine learning concepts.
