Why the models may predict differently

Logistic Regression creates a single decision boundary across the entire feature space, while Decision Tree applies a series of binary splits on individual measurements. Measurements near a class boundary can therefore lead to different predictions even with the same input.

In practice, LR treats the four measurements as a combined weighted score. DT makes discrete choices like “petal length > 2.5?” and then moves deeper into the tree. These different approaches explain why they can disagree.
